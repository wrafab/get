Week 2 - Computer-aided Design

In week 2, we learned how to build 3D models with Fusion 360. Basically, it is drawing a 2D sketch first and then extruding it in different ways to make it 3D. The first task is to build 6 models from the 50 blueprints given. They are pretty basic and can be finished easily by extruding one or several blueprints from various planes. The second task is to build a 3D model of a hinge. It is more difficult since it requires the measurement of its parts to be the same, but it can be solved by fixing the diameter of cylinders used. The assembled hinge looks like this. All parts fit each other while leaving a small space for movement. Every part in the middle of this hinge uses Revolve when creating the model, and the two flat parts use Extrude method.
