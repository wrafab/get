Week 6 - Lamination, Vacuum Forming, Molding & Casting

This week, we work on the electronics. Here, we connect the battery and motor to a breadboard and make the motor spin. Breadboard is a construction base for temporary circuits because it requires no solder. We connect a resistor and a LED in the circuit. Resistor limits the current by increasing resistance so that LED would not burn. LED is a light-emitting diode, and since it is a diode, it only conducts electricity in one direction. Another picture of resistor and LED when it is on. This is how to read a resistor. The first two bands give signficant values, the third band gives multiplying factor, and the fourth band gives tolerance range. Two LEDs are connected in parallel, meaning the circuit is broken into two paths. A LED is connected with a diode. Both components only allow current to flow in one direction, so flipping either will make the light go out. A electromagnet is connected. When introducing current through it, it becomes a magnet. A LED is connected with a switch and a capacitor. The capacitor can store electrical energy. A LED is connected with a transistor. The transistor amplifies or switches electronic signals. A LED is connected with a potentiometer. The potentiometer is a resistor that can adjust the output voltage, so it can adjust the brightness of LED. A circuit consisted of LED, capacitor, and transistor. The LED will keep turning on and off. A seven-segment display is connected. It displays numbers 0 to 9. A circuit consisted of LED, switch, and transistor. Once switch is pressed, LED will stay on even after switch is released. Another circuit of LED and transistors. There is another circuit with a microphone in it, but I forgot to take a picture of it. The next practice is to make a digital clock. To connect different parts of the clock together, we need to solder these parts on to a circuit board. We melt the soldering iron with a high temperature rod, put it on the connection of electrical parts and the board, and let it cool. The finished product. Make sure soldering iron does not go to neighbouring nods because that may cause short circuit.
